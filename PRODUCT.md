# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owners of custom homes and estates in Greater Orlando (Windermere, Isleworth, Dr. Phillips, Winter Park, Lake Nona), plus the general contractors, architects, and builders who bring CFAS onto projects, and commercial clients (offices, medical practices, multi-tenant facilities). They are affluent, time-poor, and evaluating whether this company can be trusted with a six-figure integration project. Many first encounter the site in a meeting room: proposals are reviewed on large TVs and conference displays, and referrals are checked on phones.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Central Florida Automation Services (CFAS), a smart home automation, security, and low-voltage integrator operating since 1968 with 50,000+ installations across Florida and Georgia. The site exists to convert high-value referrals into service requests and consultations, and to reassure existing clients (monitoring numbers, service requests). Success is a qualified contact-form submission or phone call.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, established, exacting. The voice is a craftsman who has done this for fifty years: plain sentences, honest trade-offs, no hype. Emotional goals: confidence, calm, the sense that "the same company answers the phone five years later." Luxury through restraint and typography, not through flash.

## Anti-references

- Big-box security brands (ADT-style urgency, alarmist red badges, "limited time offer")
- Generic SaaS landing pages (gradient heroes, floating dashboards, emoji bullets)
- Gadget-shop maximalism; the technology should recede, as it does in their installs

## Design Principles

1. **The system disappears** — like their installs, the design should feel inevitable and unnoticed; architecture (type, space, imagery) over ornament.
2. **Fifty years, not fifty widgets** — heritage and evidence (licenses, dates, real testimonials) carry the persuasion.
3. **Works the day the owner walks in** — every surface must be flawless on the device it's viewed on, from a phone in a truck to a conference-room TV.
4. **Plain words, engraved** — copy is literal and calm; typography does the expressive work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG mandate stated; site already respects `prefers-reduced-motion` throughout and uses a skip link. Maintain that, keep touch targets ≥44px, and preserve legible contrast on both the white and ink-dark page themes. Audience skews older; body text should never be small.
